My 3 Visualization Final

An analysis of my training variables and their effects on my energy levels.

Proposal 1: Time-Series (Scatterplot) Sketch 1

Hypothesis

As Active Kilocalories (Y-Axis) increase on any given day, the Energy Change (Shape) will more frequently show a negative result (downward-pointing triangle). In addition, longer workout Duration (Size) and high Maximum Heart Rate (color) might amplify the overall effort, leading to loss of Energy.

Sketch Description

A time series scatterplot with Date/Day on the X-Axis and Active Kilocalories on the Y-Axis. Each point's size represents the workout Duration and its color represents the Max Heart Rate (MaxHR). The shape is used to show the net change in energy: a triangle if the energy went up, a downward-pointing triangle if it went down, and a circle. This allows observation of high-intensity clusters over time and their correlation with the change of energy.

Data Encoding

  • X-Axis: Date/Day (Temporal/Sequential)
  • Y-Axis: Active Kilocalories (Quantitative - Intensity)
  • Color: Max Heart Rate in BPM (Sequential Gradient: Low to High Intensity)
  • Shape (Glyph): Energy Change (Post workout - Morning workout)
  • Size: Duration in min (Quantitative)

Justification

This proposal is designed to check for sequential patterns and cumulative effects. By using Date on the X-Axis, we can see if a string of high-intensity workouts (high Y-values, large size, hot color) immediately precedes a day where the energy level shape (Glyph) change after the workout. The use of Shape for the change of Energy Level provides a clear, categorical distinction of the primary result.

Proposal 2: Impact Distribution (Beeswarm) Sketch 2

Hypothesis

As the Intensity Workout (X-Axis) increases from Low to High, the median Energy Change (Y-Axis) will decrease, In addition, the Duration (Size) and Maximum Heart Rate (Color) will beassociated with data points that show the greatest negative Energy Change and reinforce the intensity to loss relationship.

Sketch Description

Three vertical columns on the X-Axis: Low Kcal, Medium Kcal, High Kcal. A horizontal line crosses the center at Y=0 (balance). The dots (circles) cluster (like a swarm) in each column based on their Y-value (energy change). I expect to see the High column's dots cluster below the zero-line. The circles are different sizes (duration) and colors (MaxHR).

Data Encoding

  • X-Axis: Intensity Workout (Kcal) (Categorical: Low, Medium, High)
  • Y-Axis:Energy Change: Post workout - Morning workout. (Quantitative, zero-centered)
  • Size: Duration in min (Quantitative)
  • Color: Max Heart Rate in BPM (Sequential: Blue to Red)

Justification

This chart is superior to a simple bar chart because it shows the full distribution and outliers, not just an average. An average could hide the fact that half the High workouts were good and the other half were bad. This visualization reveals the variability and density of the results.

Proposal 3: The Energy Journey (Flow) Sketch 3

Hypothesis

As my morning energy level increases, my end-of-day energy level increases. In addition, higher workout intensity (active kilocalories-Color) might negatively affect my end of day energy level, causing a steeper energy decline throughout the day. Furthermore, workouts with higher maximum heart rates (size) might amplify this energy decline, particularly when combined with high intensity workouts.

Sketch Description

A parallel coordinates plot with four vertical axes: Day of Week, Morning Energy, Post-Workout Energy, and End of Day Energy. Each line represents an individual workout, connecting its values across the four axes. High Kilocalories (color) and high MaxHR (thick line) rise from 'Morning' to Post-Workout, but then drop sharply on the End of Day axis.

Data Encoding

  • X-Axis: Energy (Morning Energy, After Workout Energy, End of Day Energy)
  • Y-Axis: Days and Level of Energy (Day: Monday to Friday, Energy 1-5)
  • Line: An individual workout (Nominal)
  • Line Thickness: Max Heart Rate in BPM (Quantitative)
  • Line Color: Active Kilocalories (Sequential: Blue to Red)

Justification

This is the only proposal that shows the complete journey of energy throughout the day. It allows me to see patterns in the slope of the line (loss/gain). It answers questions like: Does a good start (high morning energy) guarantee a good finish? or Does the day of the week matter?

Design Reflection and Credits

I developed these three proposals based on the design resources provided in the assignment, with the goal of finding the best way to tell my data's story.

  • Visualizing Data (Mapping Aesthetics): The Mapping Data onto Aesthetics concept (mentioned in the PDF) was the direct inspiration for Proposal 1 (Glyphs). Instead of using color to encode the energy change, I am using shape (a triangle for gain, inverse triangle for loss). This feels like a much stronger and more intuitive visual encoding for data that is inherently directional (positive or negative).
  • From Data to Viz: This resource was key for Proposal 2 (Beeswarm). Following the decision tree for one quantitative variable per category, I decided to avoid a bar chart (which hides distribution) and opt for a beeswarm plot. This allows me to see the full distribution of my results (whether the high workouts are all clustered in the negative zone, or if they are scattered) Reference Link
